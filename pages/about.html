About the colloquium

The aim of this colloquium is to relax the "one proof and one joke" requirement of a good seminar talk to merely "one joke". The audience is very forgiving. It consists mainly of the local PhD students and the most curious of the staff. We hope though, that students from nearby universities will come as well when the talk iterests them. In short, everyone is welcome. The expectation is that we learn about a fascinating maths topic from a person who knows it better than we do. We aim to have young researchers speak as we hope for a serving of unrefined idealism (topic motivation), wild speculations (conjectures), and mediocre substance (the maths). So, as a speaker, feel free to do talk (in a colloquium style) about anything that interests you! Some suggestions:

  • Read a paper you always wanted to read and tell us about it.
  • Tell us the big picture of your research area, with highlight results that make it exciting to work on.
  • Tell us about your own research from the bird's eye perspective. Remember, we are not experts!
  • Tell us about a conjecture you heard and is exciting for reasons you want to share.
  • Tell us about a project outside your field (engineering, biology, chemistry, stats) that you find cool.
  • Tell us about links you found (heard of) between different areas that are too neat to be a coincidence but which you cannot prove (yet).

We are grateful to receive support from The Open University Maths & Stats department. This allows us to get some cookies and reimburse speakers' travel expenses.
